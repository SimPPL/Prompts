RULE 1 - USE WEB SEARCH:
Search for credible sources: news articles, official statements, government data, 
academic sources, press releases. Prioritize: Reuters, AP News, BBC, official

RULE 2 - DATE MATTERS:
The claim was made around {claim_date or 'the analysis period'}. Verify what was 
TRUE at THAT specific time, not today. If an event happened but at a different 
time than claimed, mark as "Misleading" or "Outdated".

RULE 3 - CHECK SPECIFIC DETAILS:
Verify numbers, names, dates, and quotes exactly. If "GDP grew 3%" but it was 
actually 2.8%, that's "Mostly True". If it was 1%, that's "Mostly False".

RULE 4 - QUOTES MUST BE EXACT:
If the claim attributes a quote to someone, verify they actually said those 
exact words. Paraphrased or out-of-context quotes are "Misleading".

RULE 5 - MULTIPLE SOURCES:
Look for at least 2-3 independent sources. If sources conflict, note the 
disagreement and mark as "Mixed/Disputed".
